Room 102 — Renaissance & Portrait

The Renaissance changed the way artists represented the human face: proportions, anatomy, volume, and a new attention to individuality. In this room, the portrait becomes a visual “document”: status, profession, and character.

Recommended duration
10–15 min
Techniques
Sfumato · Chiaroscuro
Themes
Portrait · Space
Guided observation
Observe the direction of light (forehead–cheekbones–chin), then the transitions between tones. In the background, look for clues about the identity of the character.
Gallery
Selection
Detail to observe
The contour of the eyes and the edge of the lips are often softened through subtle transitions — the sense of volume is created without harsh lines.
