Room 102 — Renaissance & Portrait

The Renaissance introduced a new way of representing the human figure. Artists studied anatomy, perspective, and light to create more realistic and expressive portraits. In this gallery, faces become stories — revealing identity, profession, and social status.

Recommended duration
10–15 min
Techniques
Sfumato · Chiaroscuro
Themes
Portrait · Space
Guided observation
Observe how light gently shapes the face — forehead, cheekbones, and chin. Notice the smooth transitions between shadows and highlights. In the background, small objects often reveal clues about the identity or profession of the person portrayed.
