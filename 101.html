Room 101 — Medieval Art

This gallery brings together objects that speak about spiritual life, workshop craftsmanship, and the aesthetics of the medieval period: precious metals, mineral pigments, painted wood, and illuminated manuscripts. Follow the key pieces in order and observe how light and symbolism shape the visual narrative.

Recommended time
8–12 min
Route
3 highlights
Themes
Iconography · Workshops
Guided observation
Look for the fine details (engraving, gilding, outlines) and the repetition of decorative motifs.
Context
Materials were chosen for durability and prestige, but also for the clarity of their visual message.
Highlighted pieces
Highlights
  • Illuminated Gospel Book
    Parchment · tempera · metallic leaf
    Notice the decorated initials and the rhythm of the page: text, space, and image.
  • Wooden Icon
    Tempera on panel · gesso base
    The shapes are deliberate: strong outlines, controlled colors, and clear symbolic elements.
  • Fresco Fragment
    Mineral pigments · plaster
    The layering marks and pigment grain reveal the technique and the passage of time.
What to observe in this gallery
Guide

Move closer to the display cases and notice how reflections change across metal surfaces, and how volumes are built through subtle color transitions. In manuscripts, margins and initials serve as a visual “map” guiding the reader through the text.

Question for the visitor
Which detail feels the most “human” to you: the gesture, the gaze, the ornament, or the imperfection of the material?

For a better experience, keep your screen brightness at a medium level; details are easier to see without strong reflections.
